02
Sentence Structure + Grammar Patterns
These are her deliberate choices — not errors. Use them exactly as she does.

The Anaphora Stack

Repeated opening phrases build rhythm and weight
To meet yourself fully.
To hold yourself fully.
To feel yourself fully.
To witness yourself fully —
Use for: listing what a session offers, what healing feels like, what a woman will experience. Always end the stack with an em dash or a pivot sentence.

The Fragment Pair

A standalone fragment for emphasis after a full sentence
She knew this was the path I had been waiting for. Longing for.

I answered the call without hesitation.
Solo fragments are deliberate emphasis — they make the reader pause. Never "fix" them. Use after a complete sentence to land the weight.

The Em Dash Pivot

Em dash marks a breath or a turn
My body trembled — she knew.

It felt divinely aligned, this invitation to walk with the serpentine energy,
as if she had been waiting for me at the gateway of this new era of my life.
Em dash is her primary punctuation move — use it to pivot from feeling to revelation, or from image to meaning.

The Safety Anchor

One grounded, clear sentence drops into a sea of poetic language
Creating space to feel it all. To witness what lives in the tissue. To soften what has been held...

↓ then:

This heartfelt practice supports the processing of emotions, reduces pain and stagnation, and brings hydration and vitality to the breast tissue.
Every poetic passage needs one clinical-adjacent sentence to anchor trust. One is enough. It reassures the skeptic without breaking the spell for the believer.

The Invitation Close

Opens a door — never pushes through it
If you feel called, let's talk about how we can care for your beautiful breasts together 🌸

If you feel the pull — if you're ready to reconnect with your body, soften, release, and rise — please reply to this email with your embodied yes.
"If you feel called" is her signature CTA. Never pressure. Always invitation. The reader self-selects. This is intentional — she is not for everyone and the writing reflects that.

The Triple Parallel

Three matching phrases that build toward the final image
Where movement becomes prayer,
breath becomes devotion,
and the body becomes the altar of awakening.
Use for descriptions of Sensual Serpents, the transformative arc of a session, or when naming what the work offers at its fullest depth.

05
Do / Don't — Side by Side
Direct substitutions for common copy pitfalls.
✓ Say This
If you feel called, let's talk about how we can care for your beautiful breasts together.
✗ Not This
Book your breast massage session today! Limited availability.
✓ Say This
This work is for the woman who is ready to go deep — who is willing to feel what her body has been carrying.
✗ Not This
This session is perfect for anyone who wants to improve their wellness and feel their best.
✓ Say This
You do not have to live with painful periods. You do not have to live with pelvic pain. Your body holds the capacity to heal — it is waiting to be listened to.
✗ Not This
Say goodbye to painful periods with our holistic massage therapy approach!
✓ Say This
Sessions are ceremonial in nature — each one begins with smudging, grounding, and music that holds the container for the work.
✗ Not This
Stephanie offers a unique spiritual approach to massage that incorporates holistic healing rituals for a transformative experience.
✓ Say This
Stephanie is an educator and guide — not a coach. Her work does not fit into a program or a plan. It moves with you.
✗ Not This
As your women's wellness coach, Stephanie will help you achieve your health goals through a customized healing program.

Copy Formulas for Each Page Type
Structural patterns to follow when building out each section from scratch.
Service Page Opening
Name the pain she carries → Honor that she has kept going → Introduce the work as a threshold → One clinical anchor sentence → Invitation ("if you feel called")
About Page Arc
Her threshold story (turning 50, the serpent dream) → What broke open in her → The lineage she trained in → What she holds for clients → Who this is for
Sensual Serpents Page
Mythic opening — the serpent as teacher → What the journey does (concrete + poetic) → The 7 weeks as portals → Who is ready for this → The invitation
Workshop Description
One warm sentence opening (address: "Beautiful sisters" or "Beloved") → What we'll do together → Who it supports (conditions list) → Logistics (date, time, cost) → "Come as you are" close
Testimonial Pull Quote Format
Short (1–2 sentences) → Bodily or felt language → First name + city only (no last name)
07
What This Voice Is Not
Hard guardrails. If copy sounds like any of these, rewrite it.
Not "Love and Light" wellness-speak

Stephanie explicitly said: real, raw, and colorful — not superficially positive. Her work meets grief, pain, ancestral wounds, and the body's darkness. The copy must be willing to go there too.

Not clinical distance

She is not a medical provider describing procedures. She is a guide who sits across the table from you and sees you fully. Copy should feel like presence, not a brochure.

Not bullet-point wellness menus

Lists of benefits are allowed only after she has already created felt sense. Leading with bullet points flattens her voice into generic wellness marketing. Lead with story and image. Anchor with facts.

Not urgency-marketing

No "limited spots," no countdowns, no "don't miss out." Her clients self-select. The copy is a mirror — those who see themselves in it will step forward. Pressure is antithetical to her practice.

Not over-explained

"Softer, less wordy, more potent and juicy" is her own direction. Every sentence earns its place. If it can be cut without losing meaning, cut it. Density of experience — not density of words.

Not performing humility

Stephanie holds genuine authority. "Holding this space is an honor" is humble — but she also says "I am ready. Ready to share with you. Ready to guide you." She inhabits both. Copy should too.
