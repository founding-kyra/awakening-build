Subhead
Scar tissue holds memory. This work releases it.
Opening

You may not think about your cesarean scar very often. It is a thin white line — easily covered, easily forgotten.

But the body has not forgotten.

Scar tissue extends far deeper than the skin. A C-section involves multiple layers — the skin, the fascia, the muscles, the uterus itself — and the adhesions that form as each layer heals can create effects that ripple through the entire abdominal and pelvic region: numbness, pulling sensations, pain during intimacy, digestive disruption, core disconnection, a shelf of tissue that won't respond to anything you try, and a persistent sense of being cut off from your own lower body that many women describe but almost none have been offered help with.

H2
The emotional body holds this scar too.
Body

For some women, a cesarean birth was a life-saving intervention. For others, it was unexpected, or unwanted, or filled with fear they never had space to fully feel. Many women are given permission to recover physically — and no permission at all to process the birth they actually had.

The tissue holds what the mind hasn't yet completed. This work creates the space for both.

H2
What the work does.
Body

C-section scar work is a gentle, skilled process of releasing the adhesion and restriction that forms in scar tissue across multiple tissue layers. Using manual therapy, myofascial release, and castor oil treatment, this work:

Reduces tightness and restricted movement in the abdominal wall — Addresses the numbness or hypersensitivity at and around the scar itself — Supports pelvic floor function and core reconnection — Restores circulation and lymphatic flow to the area — And creates space for the emotional completion the birth may not have had room for.
